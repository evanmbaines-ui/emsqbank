## 1. Monitor Procurement Lifecycle Assessment

Question ID: `EMS2026-1-2-4-4-02`
Core content code: `1.2.4.4`
Doctrine mode: `standard`

A regional EMS agency is replacing cardiac monitors. Two shortlisted devices meet required clinical specifications but differ in purchase price, service contract, consumables, training package, data export, and expected downtime support. Which comparison best supports a defensible purchase recommendation?

A. Clinical functions, purchase price, warranty, service response time, consumables, training time, and staff familiarity, with interoperability scored after vendor selection.
B. Acquisition and service costs, consumables, training, data export, and downtime support for the first contract term, without modeling replacement-cycle costs.
C. Clinical performance, total five-year cost, training burden, current ambulance mounts, vendor support, and staff preference, with interoperability limited to existing ambulances.
D. Clinical capability, acquisition and service costs, consumables, training burden, data export, downtime support, interoperability, and replacement-cycle cost.

Answer: D

Rationale: D is best because the recommendation should compare lifecycle and operational effects, including reliability, data integration, training burden, and recurring costs. A is procurement-literate but delays interoperability until after vendor selection. B includes several important costs but limits the analysis to the first contract term and misses replacement-cycle cost. C considers five-year cost and mounts but narrows interoperability to existing ambulances and gives staff preference more weight than durable operational integration.
