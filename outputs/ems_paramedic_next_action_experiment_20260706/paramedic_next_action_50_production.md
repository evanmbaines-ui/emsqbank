An unresponsive 58-year-old is being carried down a narrow stairwell. Snoring respirations persist and the one-person bag-valve-mask seal is leaking. What should the paramedic do next?

B. Move, reposition, and reseal the mask

D. Send one rescuer to scout route

Answer: A

Rationale: A is best because blast scenes can include secondary devices and unsafe routes, so staging and command-coordinated ingress precede triage. B, C, and D commit resources forward without a confirmed safe route.

### 50. Suspected MPox Source Control
Pilot ID: `PARAMEDIC-NEXT50-050`
Question ID: `EMS2026-2-3-11-3-4-02`
Job ID: `JOB-2-3-11-3-4-02`
Core content code: `2.3.11.3.4`

A stable patient with suspected mpox needs transport. What should the paramedic do next?

A. Load patient, notify hospital en route
B. Use PPE, cover lesions, notify facility
C. Ask exposure history, rash onset, contacts
D. Document lesions, transport, notify public health

Answer: B

Rationale: B is best because suspected mpox EMS care uses PPE, lesion coverage, limited unnecessary contact, and receiving-facility notification. A and D delay protection or facility notification until transport is underway. C gathers exposure history but does not provide source control or notify the receiving facility.

Citation: Koenig KL, Bey CK, Marty AM. Monkeypox 2022: A Primer and Identify-Isolate-Inform (3I) Tool for Emergency Medical Services Professionals. Prehosp Disaster Med. 2022;37(5):687-692.

Source locator: Primary Source Materials/Gap Fill Sources/MPox/Koenig_2022_Mpox_EMS_3I_Prehosp_Disaster_Med.pdf; EMS Identify-Isolate-Inform and PPE/source-control recommendations; PDF p 5.
